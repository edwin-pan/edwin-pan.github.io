Introduction

I am a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University. I am working with Dr. Ehsan Adeli in both the Computational Neuroscience (CNS) Lab and the Stanford Vision and Learning (SVL) Lab's Video Group.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering with Highest Honors from The University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2020, along with a minor in Computer Science focused on artificial intelligence. I led a start-up company known as PreSense through acquisition as part of the Alchemy Deep Tech Incubator Program, with advising from Professor Sanjay Patel, Professor Wen-mei Hwu, and Jinjun Xiong.

My primary research interests are indisciplinary, intersecting computer vision, machine learning, and image processing for applications in both medical imaging and autonomous vehicles.

Projects

Meta-Regularization by Enforcing Mutual-Exclusiveness

@Stanford CS330: Deep Multi-Task and Meta Learning

Leveraging the meta learning paradigm opens the door for a new type of meta-overfitting known as 'memorization'. Unfortunately, clever task-construction must be proposed on a case-by-case, scenario-by-scenario basis. In this project, we propose a method of meta-regularization called Mutual-Exclusiveness Regularization (MER). MER provides a means of controlling the flow of inforamation during trainied thus inducing task mutual-exclusivity in the absence of such structure.

Generating Segmentation Masks for Autonomous Vehicle Application via Style Transfer Framework with Weakened Supervision

@UIUC CS543/ECE549: Computer Vision

This final project explores the feasibility of using a GAN based style-transfer approach to generating semantic segmentation masks for autonomous driving application. Specifically, CycleGAN and pix2pix are benchmarked against a more traditional U-Net architecture built off of Resnet-18. The project uses a subset of the image data from the ApolloScape open dataset.

Automotive mmWave FMCW Radar-based Micro-Doppler Signature Feature Extraction and Classification

@UIUC CS598PS: Machine Learning in Signal Processing

Applied CNN architecture to a micro-doppler spectrogram data set simulated through Matlab in an attempt to distinguish between a pedestrian radar motion spectrogram and a bicycle radar motion spectrogram.
